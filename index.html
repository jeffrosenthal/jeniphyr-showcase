The Big Idea

What if your server had a brain? Not just a CPU — a thinking, remembering, acting brain you could talk to from your phone.

🧠

A Brain, Not a Chatbot

Jeniphyr isn't a chat window. It's an AI that lives on your machine 24/7, with tools to interact with the real world — your files, your network, your devices. You give it hands, and it uses them.

💬

Talk From Anywhere

You talk to Jeniphyr through Telegram — the same messaging app on your phone. Send a message, get a response. No dashboards to open, no terminals to find. Just text it.

✋

Hands = Abilities

Each capability is a "Hand." File reading is a Hand. Git is a Hand. TV control is a Hand. Each assistant gets only the Hands it needs — no assistant has access to everything.

🤝

Specialists, Not Generalists

Four assistants, each with a different job. Jeniphyr handles general chat and routes. Dev handles code and devices. Research looks things up. Monitor watches the server. They delegate to each other.

How It Works

From your phone to the real world, step by step.

1

You send a Telegram message

Type something natural. "Turn off the TV." "What's the git status of proofpop?" "Remind me in 20 minutes." No special syntax needed. Prefix with /dev, /research, or /monitor to talk to a specific assistant.

2

The message reaches your server

Telegram sends a webhook to your server. The controller verifies it's really you, finds the right assistant based on any /command prefix, and drops the message into a job queue.

3

The AI thinks and acts

The queue worker picks up the job and runs the AI agent. Claude reads your message, checks its memories, decides which tools to use, and executes them — reading files, running commands, calling APIs, whatever it takes.

4

You get a response

The AI sends its answer back through Telegram. The whole loop takes a few seconds. Your conversation history is saved, so context carries over between messages.
