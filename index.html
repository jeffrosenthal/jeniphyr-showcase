The Big Idea

What if you had an AI assistant that could actually do things — not just talk, but think, remember, and act on your behalf?

An Assistant, Not a Chatbot

Siri talks. Alexa listens. Jeniphyr acts. It books things, handles email, controls your home, searches the web, manages your files, and remembers everything you tell it. It has hands — and it uses them.

Talk From Anywhere

How It Works

From your phone to the real world, step by step.

1

You send a Telegram message

Type something natural. "Turn off the TV." "What's the git status of proofpop?" "Remind me in 20 minutes." No special syntax needed. Prefix with /dev, /research, or /monitor to talk to a specific assistant.

2

The message reaches Jeniphyr

Telegram sends a webhook to the server. The controller verifies it's really you, finds the right assistant based on any /command prefix, and drops the message into a job queue.

3

The assistant thinks and acts

The queue worker picks up the job and runs the AI assistant. It reads your message, checks its memories, decides which tools to use, and executes them — reading files, running commands, calling APIs, whatever it takes.

4

You get a response

The assistant sends its answer back through Telegram. The whole loop takes a few seconds. Your conversation history is saved, so context carries over between messages.
