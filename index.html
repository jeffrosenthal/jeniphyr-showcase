What Is This?

A quick explanation for the non-technical.

🤖 Jeniphyr

Jeniphyr is a personal AI assistant that lives on a home server. You talk to it through Telegram (a messaging app), and it can do things in the real world — run commands, check websites, and now control devices.

📺 TVHand

TVHand is a new capability (called a "Hand") that gives Jeniphyr the ability to control TVs. It speaks the language of each TV brand automatically, so you just say what you want in plain English.

🔌 Driver Pattern

Different TV brands have different remote control protocols. TVHand uses a "driver" system — a Vizio driver that speaks Vizio's language, a Roku driver that speaks Roku's. Adding a new brand means adding one file.

🏠 Local Network

Everything runs on your home WiFi. The AI sends commands directly to your TV over the local network — no cloud service, no subscription, no third-party access. Your home, your control.

How It Works

From your thumbs to your TV in under a second.

1

You send a message

Open Telegram on your phone and type something natural like "turn on the TV" or "launch YouTube and bump the volume up 5."

2

Jeniphyr understands your intent

The AI figures out what you want — which device, which action, how much. No special syntax or commands to memorize.

3

TVHand picks the right driver

Based on which TV you're targeting, TVHand selects the correct driver (Vizio SmartCast, Roku ECP, etc.) and translates your request into the TV's native protocol.

4

Your TV responds

The command is sent directly over your home WiFi. The TV turns on, changes input, adjusts volume, or launches an app — and Jeniphyr confirms it in the chat.
